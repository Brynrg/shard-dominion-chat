# Shard Dominion — Game Design Document

## Elevator Pitch
Shard Dominion is a retro-modern sci-fi RTS where every economic decision changes the battlefield. Players command one of three asymmetric factions on Aether Prime, a living mineral planet whose crystalline ecology reacts to harvesting, war, power, and movement.

## Core Loop
1. Scout terrain and shard fields.
2. Deploy MCV and build Anchor Lattice.
3. Build power, processor, barracks/factory.
4. Harvest Aether Shards.
5. Defend harvesters and raid enemy harvesters.
6. Expand to better fields.
7. Manage power, lattice stability, and Planet Agitation.
8. Tech into faction identity.
9. Defeat opponent or complete mission objective.

## Strategic Tensions
- Expand fast vs. stabilize safely.
- Harvest aggressively vs. wake planet threats.
- Build raw-ground structures quickly vs. suffer corrosion.
- Maintain power surplus vs. risk storm attraction.
- Use heavy armor vs. attract Titan Leviathans.
- Raid economy vs. trigger neutral threats near yourself.

## Key Systems

### Aether Shards
The main resource. Harvesters collect from shard reefs and deliver to Processors.

Shard types:
- Blue Shards: stable, medium-low yield, low agitation.
- Red Shards: high yield, high volatility, bloom/explosion risk.
- Black Shards: rare tech resource; campaign-critical.

### Anchor Lattice
The foundation system.

Placement modes:
- Raw ground: fast, low durability, corrosion, power leakage.
- Basic lattice: normal durability, stable.
- Reinforced lattice: expensive, slow, resists storms/tremors.

### Power
Power is generated by Nodes and consumed by buildings.

Deficit effects:
- Production slows.
- Radar shuts down.
- Advanced defenses disable.
- Lattice repair slows.
- Phantom sabotage becomes stronger.

Surplus effects:
- Good: supports defenses and advanced systems.
- Bad: sudden surplus spikes increase Glass Storm chance.

### Planet Agitation
Global and local values track how disturbed the planet is.

Agitation sources:
- Harvesting.
- Heavy unit movement.
- Explosions.
- Power spikes.
- Building on raw ground.
- Thumper/decoy devices.

Planet events:
- Worm Sign.
- Titan Leviathan emergence.
- Shard Bloom.
- Glass Storm.
- Crust Collapse.
- Aether Echo false radar contacts.

### Combat
Readable rock-paper-scissors:
- Infantry beat unsupported anti-armor and capture buildings.
- Tanks beat infantry and light vehicles.
- Missiles beat armor/air but are vulnerable to infantry and fast units.
- Siege beats static defenses but is vulnerable to raids.
- Air/logistics bypass terrain but are vulnerable to anti-air and storms.

## Factions

### Vanguard Enclave
Theme: stabilization, discipline, survey control, air support.

Strengths:
- Best scouting/radar.
- Strong defensive networks.
- Reliable combined arms.
- Good air logistics.

Weaknesses:
- Predictable.
- Reliant on power and relay infrastructure.
- Less explosive economy.

Unique mechanics:
- Survey Doctrine.
- Stabilizer Drop.
- Relay-linked defenses.
- Air corridors.

### Forge Dominion
Theme: industrial survivalism, armor, extraction, pressure.

Strengths:
- Toughest vehicles.
- Strong siege.
- Best overproduction bursts.
- Salvage economy.

Weaknesses:
- Slow.
- Loud.
- High power demand.
- Generates agitation quickly.

Unique mechanics:
- Overload Foundries.
- Scrap Reclamation.
- Molten Lattice.
- Crawler Armor.

### Phantom Collective
Theme: signal cult, stealth, sabotage, post-human adaptation.

Strengths:
- Stealth and deception.
- Fast harassment.
- Hijack/disable tools.
- Low-agitation economy options.

Weaknesses:
- Fragile direct army.
- Requires scouting and micro.
- Weak static defense.

Unique mechanics:
- Signal Veil.
- Ghost Orders.
- Hijack Pulse.
- Silent Harvesters.

## Victory Conditions
Skirmish:
- Destroy all enemy Construction Yards and production structures.
- Optional alternative: control all Relay Towers for X minutes.
- Optional alternative: extract Black Shard objective.

Campaign:
- Objective-driven missions.
- Optional objectives unlock doctrines, codex entries, or variant units.
