# Shard Dominion — Narrative and World Bible

## Setting
Aether Prime is a hostile exoplanet covered in crystalline mineral reefs, black glass deserts, red storm basins, and buried ruins from a failed human colony.

The Aether Shards are not just fuel. They store resonance patterns, memories, and possibly consciousness. The first colony discovered this too late.

Decades after colony silence, a repeating transmission reaches human space:

> Do not mine the dead. We are still here.

Three factions arrive to claim, stabilize, exploit, or commune with Aether Prime.

## Tone
- Strategic military sci-fi.
- Ancient mystery without fantasy magic.
- Environmental dread.
- Faction propaganda vs. hidden truth.
- Harsh but colorful 80s/90s sci-fi energy.

## Faction Voices

### Vanguard Enclave
Voice: formal, controlled, mission-first.
Sample announcer lines:
- Anchor lattice established.
- Survey data confirms unstable crust.
- Power deficit. Command systems degraded.
- Civilian colony records recovered. Stand by for decryption.

### Forge Dominion
Voice: blunt, industrial, proud, fatalistic.
Sample lines:
- Foundry hot.
- Shards in the hold.
- Armor column rolling.
- Let the planet scream. We need that field.

### Phantom Collective
Voice: quiet, layered, signal-corrupted, philosophical.
Sample lines:
- We are unseen.
- Their machines are listening to us now.
- The shard remembers your name.
- Do not silence the signal.

### Planet / Aether Echo
Voice: fragmentary, human and inhuman.
Sample lines:
- We were miners.
- We were children.
- You walk on our sleep.
- Leave the black shard buried.

## Campaign Structure
Nine core missions, three per faction.

### Act I — Landfall
1. Vanguard: First Lattice
2. Forge: Claim the Red Field
3. Phantom: Voices in the Dust

### Act II — Escalation
4. Vanguard: The Worm Road
5. Forge: Furnace Line
6. Phantom: Borrowed Machines

### Act III — Revelation
7. Vanguard: Stabilization Protocol
8. Forge: The Sleeping Engine
9. Phantom: Dominion Shard

## Endings
- Extraction Victory: humanity survives by consuming Aether Prime.
- Stabilization Victory: colony is restored, but the Aether intelligence is contained.
- Convergence Victory: humans and Aether merge into a new distributed mind.
- Planet Victory: over-agitation causes Titan ecology to erase all factions.
- Hidden Accord: optional objectives reveal a coexistence path.
