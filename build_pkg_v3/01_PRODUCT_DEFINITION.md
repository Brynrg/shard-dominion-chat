# Product Definition

## Product promise

Shard Dominion is a single-player, retro-modern science-fiction RTS set on Aether Prime, a living
mineral world that reacts to extraction, movement, construction, power usage, and combat.

The game must deliver:

- resource harvesting;
- vulnerable logistics;
- base construction;
- power management;
- fog-of-war;
- scouting;
- unit production;
- unit counters;
- asymmetric factions;
- neutral planetary threats;
- skirmish AI;
- campaign missions;
- briefings and debriefings;
- save/progression;
- original art and audio.

## Scope target

### Campaign

- 9 missions minimum;
- 3 acts;
- 3 playable faction perspectives across the campaign;
- optional objectives;
- at least 3 campaign choices;
- 5 endings;
- local save/progress.

### Skirmish

- 1v1 player versus AI;
- 4 maps minimum;
- 3 difficulty levels;
- annihilation victory;
- optional secondary victory mode after launch candidate.

### Factions

- Vanguard Enclave;
- Forge Dominion;
- Phantom Collective.

### Units

Minimum launch target: 27 unit definitions.

- 10 shared/basic functional roles;
- 5 unique or strongly faction-modified units per faction;
- 1 apex unit per faction;
- air/logistics represented;
- engineers/capture represented;
- harvesters represented.

### Buildings

Minimum launch target: 18 building definitions including faction variants.

### Planet systems

Launch requires:

- Shard Bloom;
- Glass Storm;
- Crust Collapse;
- Sensor Echoes;
- Titan Worm emergence.

## Quality bar

The game is not considered complete if it only has:

- one working mission;
- placeholder circles and rectangles;
- no AI;
- no save;
- no campaign flow;
- no proper menus;
- no production system;
- no faction asymmetry;
- no audio;
- no final art;
- no release testing.

## IP distance

The game may be inspired by late-1990s RTS design principles, but must not copy:

- faction names;
- unit names;
- building names;
- mission scripts;
- UI layout;
- art direction;
- lore;
- dialogue;
- music;
- sound effects;
- silhouettes;
- logos;
- map layouts;
- cinematics;
- marketing language.
